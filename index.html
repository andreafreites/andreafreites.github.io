Andrea Freites

Andrea Freites is a PhD candidate in Political Science (International Relations and Research Methods) at the Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile and as a doctoral researcher at the Millennium Nucleus on the Impacts of China in Latin America and the Caribbean (ICLAC). She holds an M.A. in Political Science from Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile, an M.A. in International Studies (Highest Distinction) from the University of Chile, a Master’s degree in Values and Citizenship Education from the Universidad Católica Andrés Bello, and a B.A. in Education from Universidad Central de Venezuela.

Her research examines how emerging powers—particularly China—interact with state institutions in developing economies, with a focus on environmental governance, extractive industries, and regulatory compliance in Latin America. Substantively, her work speaks to debates in Global Political Economy, International Relations and Comparative Environmental Politics. Methodologically, she employs quantitative and mixed-methods approaches.

Andrea has published in international peer-reviewed journals, including The Extractive Industries and Society, Latin American Journal of Asian Studies, Revista Estudios Internacionales, and International Journal on Homelessness, with ongoing work under review at the Journal of Environment and Development and the Review of International Political Economy.

She has teaching experience at Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile and the University of Chile and currently serves as Editorial Manager of the Revista de Ciencia Política. For inquiries, Andrea Freites can be reached at apfreites@uc.cl
