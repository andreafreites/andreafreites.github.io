About Me

Andrea Freites is a PhD candidate in Political Science (International Relations and Research Methods) at the Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile and a doctoral researcher at the Millennium Nucleus on the Impacts of China in Latin America and the Caribbean (ICLAC). She holds an M.A. in Political Science from PUC, an M.A. in International Studies (Highest Distinction) from the University of Chile, a Master’s in Values and Citizenship Education, and a B.A. in Education from Universidad Central de Venezuela.

Research

Her research examines how emerging powers, particularly China, interact with state institutions in developing economies, with a focus on environmental governance, extractive industries, and regulatory compliance in Latin America. She works on Global Political Economy, International Relations, and comparative environmental politics using quantitative and mixed-methods approaches.

Teaching

Experience includes International Relations Theory, Asian Studies, Quantitative Methods, and more.
