Current Research

My dissertation examines the interaction between Chinese foreign direct investment and environmental governance in South America's extractive sector, with a focus on Argentina, Chile, and Peru. It investigates the conditions under which host-country regulatory frameworks shape the environmental performance of Chinese-owned firms, contributing to debates on the political economy of Chinese overseas investment and comparative environmental governance.

I am affiliated with the Millennium Nucleus on the Impacts of China in Latin America and the Caribbean (ICLAC), where I contribute to collaborative projects on Chinese investment patterns, green transitions, and socio-environmental governance in the region.
